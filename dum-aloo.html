Dum Aloo

(Vegetarian)

We all love to eat 'aloor dom' along with 'luchi' or 'kochuri'. This is our comfort food. It also goes very well with 'pulao' or 'fried rice'. This one is my mom's recipe. She make the tastiest aloor dom ever. Yesterday I tried this recipe and it came out perfectly.Just give it a try.




Ingredients


  • 10 pieces of baby potatoes.
  • 1 onion thinly sliced.
  • 1 tsp of ginger and garlic paste.
  • 1 tomato finely chopped.
  • 3 green chilies.
  • A handfull of green peas.
  • Whole spices(1 small cinnamon stick, 2 bay leaves, 4 green cardamoms, 4 cloves, 1 tsp cumin seeds).
  • 2 tsp turmeric powder.
  • 1 tsp red chili powder.
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder.
  • 1 tsp roasted coriander powder.
  • Salt and sugar.
  • 4 tsp mustard oil.
  • 2 tsp ghee / clarified butter.
  • Warm water.

Steps


  • Half boil the baby potatoes with some salt and peeled them. Keep aside.
  • Take a pan. Heat mustard oil.
  • Add the peeled potatoes and 1 tsp of turmeric powder in the hot oil. Fry them for 4-5 minutes. The potatoes will get a nice color.
  • Remove them from oil and keep aside.
  • In the remaining oil add 2 tsp ghee.
  • Add the whole spices and 2 tsp sugar into the hot oil. Saute them for a minute. Caramelize the sugar.
  • Now add thinly sliced onion. Fry them untill golden brown.
  • Add ginger and garlic paste. Mix it for a minute.
  • Add chopped tomato, salt, 1 tsp turmeric powder and green chilies. Cook this in slow flame for 5-6 minutes.
  • When the tomato become mushy add red chili powder, roasted cumin and coriander powder. Mix this very well for 5 minutes.
  • When the oil starts separating add fried baby potatoes. Mix again for 4 minutes.
  • Now add some warm water and green peas. Cook this untill the potatoes are 100% done.
  • Check the seasoning and adjust it according to your taste.
  • Turn off you gas.

Your Aloor Dum is ready ...
  • Enjoy this chilled drink with some whipped cream on top ...
