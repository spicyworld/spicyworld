Chicken Tandoori

(Non-Vegetarian)

This dish dont need any explanation. Its very popular in India and nowadays all over the world. There was a rumor that without "tandoor/oven" you cannot make tandoori. But believe me today I am sharing "oven method & gas method" of making tandoori. Please give it a try at your home and prove the rumor wrong.




Ingredients


  • 4 Chicken leg pieces.
  • 6 tsp hung curd.
  • Salt.
  • 2 tsp fresh ginger & garlic paste.
  • 3 tsp lemon juice.
  • 4 tsp mustard oil.
  • 5 tsp store bought tandoori masala or 1 tsp (cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, black pepper powder, kashmiri mirch powder, garam masala powder, dry fenugreek leave powder).
  • Some butter for basting.

Steps


  • Initial Steps.
  • Wash the chicken pieces and make some slits with sharp knife. Pat dry the chicken.
  • Add some salt, ginger & garlic paste and lemon juice to the chicken. Mix this very well with your hand. Keep it aside for 30 minutes.
  • Now take a big bowl. Add mustard oil.
  • Then add 1 tsp red chili powder and 1 tsp kashmiri red chili powder. Mix it and let it rest for 4-5 minutes. From this you will get nice red color.
  • Then add all the powder 1 tsp each in the oil. Mix it.
  • If you are using store bought powder, just put the powder in oil and let it rest for 5 minutes.
  • After that put hung curd and salt. Mix it very well.
  • Now add the chicken pieces. Massage the chicken pieces with masala very well with your hand. Cover the bowl and keep it in refrigerator overnight or atleast 4-5 hours.
  • Oven method
  • Preheat your oven at the highest temperature for 10-15 minutes.
  • Now take a grill pan. Place the matinated chicken pieces and put in the oven for 20 minutes in 450F.
  • Then bast some butter on the both side. Turn the chicken pieces on other side and follow the same procedure.
  • Turn off your oven and let it rest for another 5 minutes.
  • If you need more juicy chicken bast some more butter on top.
  • Gas method
  • Take a iron skillet / pan. Heat it very well.
  • Put very little oil and spread all over the hot pan.
  • Now put the chicken pieces in the hot pan. Dont overcrowd the pan.
  • Cook each side for 15-20 minutes in medium high flame. Bast some butter in between.
  • To get the smokey flavour turn your burner and hold the chicken pieces in direct flame each side for 1 minute.

Your chicken tandoori is ready ...
  • Sprinkle some lemon juice and enjoy this snack with mint chutney & onion rings.
