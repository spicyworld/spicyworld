Singara (samosa)

(Vegetarian)

In India samosa is a very popular appetizer. We call it "singara" in West Bengal. Almost every evening my grandpa brought samosas from roadside shop and they were delicious. Last week I made samosas at home (inspired by my mother & husband) and they came out really tasty. So, please try this recipe at your home and impress your family.




Ingredients


  • 2 large boiled, peeled potatoes.
  • Some boiled green peas.
  • 2 cups all purpose flour.
  • 3-4 tsp ghee / clarified butter.
  • Salt to taste.
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds.
  • Half tsp chopped ginger.
  • 1 tsp chopped green chilies.
  • 1 tsp cumin powder.
  • 1 tsp coriander powder.
  • 1 tsp dry mango powder.
  • 1 tsp red chili powder.
  • Some chopped coriander leaves.
  • White oil.
  • Some warm water.

Steps


  • Dough:
  • Take 2 cups of flour in a bowl.
  • Add some salt and 4 tsp melted ghee. Mix all this with your hand for 5 minutes.
  • Then add little warm water into flour and mix it. The dough should be semi soft. So, do not add water at a time. Go for little by little.
  • Knead the dough very well for 10 minutes. Cover the dough with a damp cloth for 20 minutes.
  • Filling:
  • Make sure you boil the potatoes in little water. If the potatoes absorb water it will ruin the dish.
  • Half mash the boiled potatoes with your hand. Dont make it mushy.
  • Take a pan and heat 2 tsp oil.
  • Add cumin seeds in hot oil. Saute it for a minute.
  • Then add chopped ginger and green chilies. Saute it for 2 minutes.
  • Add the half mashed potatoes. Saute it for a minute.
  • Then add all the powder and some salt. Mix it very well for 5 minutes.
  • Check the seasoning.
  • Add boiled peas and coriander leaves. Mix it again for 3 minutes.
  • Keep this mixture aside and let it cool down completely.
  • Process:
  • Now knead the dough again for just a minute and make medium size balls from it.
  • Flatten a ball to a 6 inch circle. The circle should not be too much thin.
  • Take a knife and cut the circle to 2 semi circles.
  • Now put 1 semi circle on your palm. Dip your fingure into water and apply water along the edges.
  • Take one end in the center and overlap it to the another end for making a cone.
  • Put the potato mixture into the cone. Seal the edges properly.
  • Deep fry the samosas into hot oil untill golden brown.
  • Oil should be medium hot. You have to fry it for approx 10-12 minutes. Otherwise it will not become crunchy.

Your Singara is ready ...
  • Enjoy this hot as appetizer ...
